Told as a remembrance, a chronicled, episodic, coming of age, dark-comedy. It spans the 50s and 60s and incorporates music of the era. Unconventional character driven and situation dependent, the stories' vignettes comment on family, love, loneliness, communicating and surviving. Influenced by the film Breakfast At Tiffanys, teenager Janice, aspires to be Holly-Go-Lightly.
