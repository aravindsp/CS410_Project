 A Corner of Her Eye (2005 Video)211) Three brothers ride out Hurricane Katrina in their father's house in Bay St. Louis, Mississippi. An intimate, spooky family portrait that starts as a father's adventurous challenge and rapidly turns to a fight for survival. Spoilers The synopsis below may give away important plot points. Three brothers from New York visit their father in Bay St. Louis, Mississippi and find themselves in the direct path of Hurricane Katrina as she approaches. The gripping and visually stunning moment-by-moment experience of three young men who take on a hurricane as a personal challenge, influenced by their father. Though their stepmother offers the brothers shelter in a military base, they choose to ride out the storm in their father's house. But father leaves to help the local police during the storm, so the brothers find themselves alone as Katrina makes landfall. The camera's POV roves among the brothers to offer a visceral experience of Katrina's escalating fury. The storm surge is the tangible monster of the story, at first only glimpsed, then encroaching, then overwhelming. The brothers' cockiness gives way to to humility as they realize what they're up against. Though the father is seen only in glimpses, his powerful emotional influence steadily accumulates along with the Katrina's fury, coming to the foreground in the aftermath of the storm, when the brothers are forced to ask why their father chose to leave them in the house alone. This documentary was edited from first-hand footage of this history-making storm, its raw verite style punctuated with intimate family insights elaborated through interviews. The musical score by guitar vituoso Jack Rose encompasses multi-hued layers of contemplation in an American primitive folk style.
